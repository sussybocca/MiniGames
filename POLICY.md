# MiniGames Feature Request & Update Policy

We love hearing from our community! This document outlines how you can request new features and what to expect from us.

## How to Request a Feature

1. **Open an Issue**  
   Go to our [GitHub Issues page](https://github.com/sussybocca/MiniGames/issues) and create a new issue with the label "enhancement". Please describe your idea in detail – what it does, why it's useful, and any implementation ideas you have.

2. **Be Patient**  
   We are a small team and do our best to review all requests. We may ask clarifying questions or discuss alternatives.

3. **Upvote Existing Requests**  
   If a feature you want is already requested, add a 👍 reaction to the issue. This helps us gauge interest.

## What Happens Next

- **If your feature is added** – We'll implement it as time and resources permit. When it's ready, we'll close the issue and mention it in the release notes.
- **If your feature is not added** – It means we either:
  - Ran out of time while developing it and it didn't make the cut.
  - Encountered technical difficulties that made it unworkable.
  - Determined it doesn't fit the project's direction.

  In any case, we'll update the issue explaining why it wasn't implemented. You're free to discuss or suggest alternatives.

## No Guarantee

While we strive to add popular and feasible features, **we cannot guarantee that every request will be implemented**. The final decision rests with the maintainers.

## Updates and Forks

- We will periodically release updates to the official repository. If you have forked the project, you are responsible for merging updates (subject to the terms of the LICENSE).
- If we discover unauthorized modifications of public instances, we will follow the takedown process described in the LICENSE.

Thank you for being part of MiniGames! Your feedback helps us make the project better.
